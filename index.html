Hyacynth


I used to associate the spectrum of purple hues

and the delicate tints of violets with Sappho. I think

about sapphic love, a love condemned for simply being,

for flourishing between women in a world unready to embrace it.

How the love I give is deemed as sinful. A bit of a negative connotation

connotation that clings to it, hmm? As if love could ever be

wrong for simply existing as we are.


Now, the sight of those shades always bring you to mind.

I think about your eyes—how they look at me. I picture your lips

and how I could spend hours tracing the curve of them with my thoughts.

I think about the warmth in your laugh, how it lingers in the air

long after you've gone. I think of you. Your name. Hyacynth.


And somehow, my thoughts aren't just confined to my mind anymore.

I seek your color in every tone I come across, as if it has always belonged to you.

I search for the flower that bears your name, as though nature itself had intended to craft a bloom as breathtaking as you to name it in your honor.

And with every encounter, I find myself more certain that the universe, in its own subtle way, was always meant to reflect the essence of you.


What was once a symbol of something suppressed has become the embodiment of everything I adore.

These hues no longer carry shame or silence, but instead a quiet, unyielding joy, a celebration of everything you are to me.


Always thinking of you,

Dom
